# How Vancelian distributes a stable rate — fixed rates, Cloud Mining exception, and the smoothing margin

## Short answer

The rates Vancelian distributes on Vaults (Flexible, Future) and on Exclusive Offers are **fixed**. The only exception is **Cloud Mining**, where the rate is **contractually variable** because the reward depends on hashing performance and BTC market conditions. When the underlying allocation evolves or a source of yield contracts, small variations in the gross yield generated by the allocation can be absorbed by **Vancelian's intermediation margin** — so that the rate paid to clients remains stable.

## Details

### Fixed rates on Vaults and Exclusive Offers

Vancelian distributes a **fixed indicative rate** on Vaults (Flexible, Future) and on real estate Exclusive Offers (Dubai Villa Al Barari, Munduk Premium Villa Bali, future projects). The rate visible in the app at the moment of subscription applies for the duration of your engagement, regardless of small variations in the underlying allocation. Your distributed rate is contractual.

### Cloud Mining is the exception — variable by contract

Cloud Mining is the only product distributed by Vancelian where the rate is **contractually variable**. The reward is tied to the mining performance and BTC market conditions. This is set out in the CGUPM (Conditions Générales d'Utilisation des Programmes de Mining) and visible in the offer documentation. See [[cloud-mining-yield-factors]].

### Why a smoothing mechanism is needed

A Vault allocation evolves daily. The management committee rebalances between the EURC liquidity pocket and the yield-generating products (Cloud Mining, Exclusive Offers) based on liquidity needs and the performance of each underlying. When a source of yield is added, contracted, or replaced, the **gross yield generated by the allocation can vary slightly**. Without a smoothing mechanism, this variation would be passed through to the client and the distributed rate would fluctuate.

### Where the margin sits — and why it acts as a buffer

Vancelian operates as the regulated intermediation platform: it selects the products, structures the offer, manages the allocation, and operates the custody. **A portion of the gross yield is retained as Vancelian's intermediation margin** — this is Vancelian's business model.

That same margin acts as a **buffer**. When small variations occur on the gross yield, the buffer absorbs them so that the rate distributed to clients remains the rate they subscribed at. The client sees a stable rate; the underlying variation is taken on the margin side.

This is true for both Vaults and Exclusive Offers (excluding Cloud Mining, which follows its variable contractual rule).

## Caveats

- Rates are **indicative and may change between subscription windows** — always check the live rate in the Vancelian app before opening or topping up a position.
- The buffer absorbs **small variations** linked to allocation rebalancing or a contraction of one yield source. It is not designed to absorb a structural change in the underlying market: in that case Vancelian may publish a new indicative rate for new subscriptions, while existing positions keep the rate they subscribed at, in line with the offer terms.
- Cloud Mining rate is **variable by contract** and is not subject to this smoothing mechanism — its rate moves with mining performance.
- For specific questions on the rate of a position you already hold, contact a Vancelian advisor.
