# Vancelian regulatory roadmap — from PSAN to digital private bank

## Summary

Vancelian (brand of Automata Holding ADGM group, formerly Akt.io / Rayn) pursues a progressive licensing strategy across two jurisdictions — **France** (via Automata France SAS) and **UAE/Dubai** (via Automata FZE). The ambition by 2030 is to converge toward a **next-generation digital private banking** model covering digital assets, electronic money, payment services, traditional financial instruments, and asset management.

The roadmap is structured in three phases: Foundations (2026), Independence (2027–2028), and Digital Private Bank (2028–2030).

> **This page is internal reference.** Client-facing status should always be drawn from the [regulation FAQ page](../faq/legal-compliance/where-and-how-is-vancelian-regulated.md).

## Group legal structure

| Entity | Jurisdiction | Current Status (April 2026) | Target Licenses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Automata France SAS** | France | PSAN Registered (AMF E2023-087) | MiCA → EMI → MiFID 2 → Simplified Credit Institution |
| **Automata FZE** | UAE (Dubai) | VARA IPA obtained (Dec 2025) | VARA → Agent VARA → SVF → Cat 3 AM → Banking License |

**Holding:** Vancelian Group ADGM (Abu Dhabi) — 100% owns both entities.

**Legal firms:** De Gaulle Fleurance + D&A Partners (Europe), W3C (UAE).

## Phase 1 — Foundations (H1-H2 2026)

### Europe (Automata France)

**MiCA / PSCA** — Crypto-Asset Service Provider
- Regulator: AMF
- File status: submitted, under review
- Regulatory deadline: **June 30, 2026** (end of PSAN transition period)
- Target obtainment: **June 2026**
- Services: custody, crypto exchange (fiat ↔ crypto, crypto ↔ crypto), order execution, order reception/transmission, advice, portfolio management + staking
- Coverage: all EU/EEA via passporting
- Impact: continuity of French activity

**EMI** — Electronic Money Institution
- Regulator: ACPR (Bank of France)
- Regulatory base: Directive 2009/110/CE (EMD2)
- File submission: **end June 2026**
- Target obtainment: **June-July 2027**
- Required capital: 250,000 € minimum
- Total estimated cost: ~500k-800k € (legal, compliance, capital, audit, tech)
- Impact: internalize IBAN issuance, multi-currency accounts, direct Visa/Mastercard access, eliminate ModulR BaaS dependency, enable crypto-collateralized fiat loans (crypto-Lombard)

**MiFID 2 Agent** — Traditional Financial Instruments
- Phase 1: Tied Agent under Assetera (or equivalent), target launch **September-October 2026**
- Products: ETFs, equities, bonds, commodities, precious metals
- Cost: negligible (no own license, no dedicated capital)
- Phase 2: Own MiFID 2 license, file submission **Q4 2026**, target obtainment **Q4 2027 / Q1 2028**

**CIF** — Financial Investment Advisor (Conseiller en Investissements Financiers)
- Regulated by: AMF / ORIAS
- Model: acquisition of existing CIF client base (Wealthy Gestion Privée, ~23-25 M€ AuM)
- New entity: "Automata CIF" — dedicated subsidiary
- Valuation: ~500 k€ (share swap for equity)
- Founder Alexandre Monteuil retains operational leadership + 20% stake
- Timeline: post-fundraising **(H2 2026 / H1 2027)**
- Impact: traditional wealth management (life insurance, securities accounts, structured products, real estate club deals, private equity)

### UAE (Automata FZE)

**VARA** — Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority
- Status: **IPA (In-Principle Approval) obtained December 2025**, full license imminent
- Legal counsel: W3C
- App launch: **May 1, 2026** (pending full license)
- Services: Broker Dealer, Management & Investments, Lending & Borrowing, Advisory
- Impact: among the first VARA-regulated players with consumer app in Dubai

**Agent VARA** — B2B Crypto Infrastructure
- Application: **April 2026** (once VARA license operational)
- Target operational: **September-October 2026**
- Model: partners operate under Vancelian's VARA license (file fees + monthly sponsorship)
- Target: crypto/fintech companies wanting to operate in Dubai without own VARA license
- Impact: recurring B2B revenue line

**Cat 3 Agent** — Asset Manager Agent (traditional stock exchange)
- Target launch: **October-November 2026** (agent of licensed UAE Asset Manager)
- Products: traditional stock exchange on Vancelian Dubai app

## Phase 2 — Independence (2027-2028)

| Date | License | Geo | Milestone |
|---|---|---|---|
| H2 2026/H1 2027 | CIF | FR | CIF authorization → +23-25 M€ AuM |
| End Q1 2027 | Cat 3 AM | UAE | Cat 3 file submission (Central Bank) |
| Jun-Jul 2027 | EMI | FR | EMI license obtained → own IBAN, cards, multi-currency |
| Sep-Nov 2027 | EMI | FR | EMI services launch → end of ModulR dependency |
| Q4 2027 | MiFID 2 | FR | Own MiFID 2 license obtained → traditional exchange independence |
| End 2027/Beg. 2028 | SVF | UAE | SVF license obtained → UAE payments & cards, end Zand Bank dependency |
| Q1-Q2 2028 | Agent SVF | UAE | Agent SVF operational → B2B fiat+crypto infrastructure for partners |
| Q1-Q2 2028 | Cat 3 AM | UAE | Cat 3 Asset Management obtained → independent UAE asset management |

**SVF (Stored Value Facility)** — UAE equivalent of EMI
- Regulator: Central Bank of the UAE
- File submission: **November-December 2026**
- Target obtainment: **end 2027 / beginning 2028**
- Required capital: 15,000,000 AED (~3.1 M€)
- Services: payment accounts, transfers, card issuance, multi-currency
- Impact: replace Zand Bank as fiat partner, unique VARA + SVF positioning in Dubai

**Agent SVF** — B2B Neo-banking Infrastructure (NymCard model)
- Prerequisite: SVF license obtained
- Operational: **Q1-Q2 2028**
- Offering: plug & play crypto + fiat in B2B (VARA + Agent SVF combined)
- Pricing: ~2,000 €/month vs ~125,000 €/year at traditional bank

> **Europe-UAE Convergence:** by back-to-school 2028, the group will have the same product delivery capacity (crypto + fiat + traditional stock exchange + asset management) across both geographies.

## Phase 3 — Digital Private Bank (2028-2030)

**Simplified Credit Institution (France)**
- Regulator: ACPR / ECB
- File launch: **Q1 2029**
- Estimated duration: 2-3 years
- Target obtainment: **by end of 2030**
- Prerequisites: MiCA + EMI + MiFID 2 obtained, sufficient recurring revenues
- Benchmark: Revolut (Europe) / Wio (UAE)
- Impact: "bank" label, credit access, premium positioning

**UAE Banking License**
- Regulator: Central Bank of the UAE
- Prerequisite: SVF + Cat 3 AM obtained, recurring revenues and/or Series B
- Estimated launch: **post-SVF — to be determined based on financial capacity**
- Impact: banking status in both jurisdictions → next-generation digital private bank

> At this stage of the business plan (2026-2028), the banking licenses are documented ambitions but not budgeted. They will be redefined in due course based on revenues and capitalization.

## Key differentiator: tokenization engine (2027 horizon)

One of the group's primary differentiation factors is integrated engineering (tech + legal + regulatory) designed to enable the **tokenization of unlisted private assets or those with limited liquidity** by 2027. This infrastructure aims to match liquidity sources with private asset financing needs (Decentralized Protocol) within a structured, scalable framework — a natural extension of the Vault wealth management division.

## Risks & dependencies

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| MiCA approval delay (>30/06/2026) | Low | Critical | File already submitted; close AMF follow-up; Plan B = request extension |
| EMI rejection or additional conditions | Medium | High | ACPR pre-engagement; De Gaulle Fleurance + D&A Partners |
| Compliance team overload (EMI + MiFID 2) | Medium | Medium | Sequential file scheduling; same firms sequentially |
| SVF Central Bank UAE processing delay | Medium | High | Prudent margin (Nov-Dec 2026 instead of Sep); VARA advocacy |
| SVF capital requirement (15M AED) | Medium | High | Initial fundraising + 2027 revenues |
| CIF acquisition failure | Low | Low | Multiple targets identified; alternative = creation from scratch |
