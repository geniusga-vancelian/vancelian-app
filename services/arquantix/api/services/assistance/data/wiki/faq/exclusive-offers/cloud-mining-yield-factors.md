# What drives Cloud Mining yields and why aren't they guaranteed?

## Short answer
The yield is **variable and never guaranteed**. It depends on four moving parts: the **Bitcoin price**, the **network difficulty**, the **cost of energy**, and the **efficiency of the machines**. The 2025 industry reference, the **hashprice**, sits around **0.04–0.05 USD per TH/s per day** — equivalent to roughly **11–13% gross annualised yield** at the market level (before operating costs and operator margins). The actual **net yield paid to Vancelian investors** is set per Privilege Club tier and shown in the app — see the [[../memberships/privilege-club-tier-benefits|Privilege Club tier benefits]] page for the live figure.

## Details

### Why no operator can guarantee a fixed rate
A guaranteed fixed rate would mean the operator absorbs all the risk of BTC price drops, difficulty increases, and energy cost spikes — which is structurally impossible for a real mining business. Any platform offering a guaranteed mining yield is a red flag (see [[cloud-mining-is-it-a-scam|Is Cloud Mining a scam?]]).

### The hashprice — the industry's benchmark metric
**Hashprice** = gross daily revenue per unit of computing power (USD per TH/s per day). It moves with two variables:
- **Bitcoin price** ↑ → hashprice ↑
- **Network difficulty** ↑ → hashprice ↓

When difficulty rises faster than the BTC price, the hashprice falls — and so do mining yields across the entire industry, not just at Vancelian.

### Why difficulty keeps rising
Bitcoin's protocol re-targets difficulty every **2,016 blocks (~2 weeks)** to maintain a 10-minute average block time. As more efficient machines join the network, difficulty rises mechanically — the share of reward per unit of hashrate falls for everyone.

### Other macro influences in 2024–2025
- **Bitcoin ETFs** — institutional capital now flows directly into BTC ETFs, partly bypassing miners. This intensifies miner competition.
- **AI vs mining** — AI compute demand competes with Bitcoin mining for the same energy and infrastructure resources, putting upward pressure on electricity prices.
- **Halving cycles** — every ~4 years the block subsidy is cut in half. See [[cloud-mining-bitcoin-halving-impact|Bitcoin halving impact]].

### Why the offer remains attractive despite sector-wide compression
- **No hardware to manage** — full operator service.
- **Daily rewards in EURC** (euro-pegged stablecoin), credited automatically.
- **Aligned with global mining returns** — Vancelian's offer tracks the international hashprice market rather than diverging from it.
- **Long-term stabilisation lever** — Hearst retains a portion of mined BTC, betting that Bitcoin's structural scarcity (capped supply at 21M, halvings) supports prices over the cycle, partly offsetting yield compression.

## Caveats
- The advertised rate is **always indicative**. Verify the live figure in the Vancelian app.
- The market hashprice (~11–13% gross at end-2025) is **not** the rate paid to investors. The investor's **net rate** (currently 2.65%–3% depending on Privilege Club tier per existing wiki references) is set after operating costs and Hearst/Vancelian margins.
- Cloud Mining is **not a fixed-income product**. Treat it as a long-horizon, variable-yield exposure to Bitcoin mining productivity.
