# Will the AKTIO and future VNC tokens be MiCA-compliant?

> **Regulatory escalation:** the chatbot must escalate any specific token-classification, tax, or regulatory question to a Vancelian human advisor.

## Short answer
Yes, the future VNC token is being designed as a MiCA-compliant utility token with a validated whitepaper, mandatory disclosures, and governance controls. Longer term, Vancelian may transform VNC into a security token backed by company valuation, which would shift it to EU financial markets law (MiFID II, Prospectus) rather than MiCA.
## Details

### Token classification under MiCA — the simplified decision tree
Under MiCA's framework, every crypto-asset must be classified before issuance:
1. **Is it a financial instrument under MiFID II?** (economic / political rights, negotiability, share class) → **Out of MiCA**; full traditional financial regime applies (MiFIR / Prospectus / MAR / UCITS / AIFMD).
2. **Is it an EMT (E-Money Token)?** (1:1 reference to an official currency) → MiCA EMT rules + EMD2 (issuance reserved to credit institutions or e-money institutions).
3. **Is it an ART (Asset-Referenced Token)?** (basket of assets / other reference) → MiCA ART rules (authorisation, reserves, audits, governance).
4. **Otherwise** → "Other crypto-assets" — generic MiCA framework (utility tokens, payment tokens, etc.).

### How VNC will be classified
The future **VNC** is being designed to qualify as an **utility token** under MiCA. This requires:
- A **whitepaper validated by the relevant national authority**.
- Mandatory information disclosures on rights conferred, risks, technology, sustainability indicators, allocation, governance, and conflicts of interest.
- Compliance with conduct rules (no misleading marketing, transparent communication, conflict management).
- Documentation traceability and integrity.

### The longer-term security-token path
Vancelian envisages — at a later stage — a transformation of VNC into a **security token** backed by the group's valuation. This is a much heavier regulatory step:
- The token would shift from MiCA to **MiFID II / Prospectus / CSDR** scope.
- It would require a **prospectus approved by a competent authority**.
- A **recognised central depositary** would be required.
- The token would be subject to standard financial market rules (MAR market abuse, UCITS / AIFMD where applicable).
- Additional licences covering the tokenisation of financial assets (shares, bonds, RWAs) would be necessary.

### Why this matters for current AKTIO holders and future VNC investors
- The migration AKTIO → VNC is **specifically designed to anticipate MiCA**, giving holders a fully regulation-aligned token from day one.
- Holders will benefit from the strengthened transparency and investor protection that MiCA brings.
- The longer-term security-token transformation is a strategic option, not a commitment — its execution depends on Vancelian obtaining the required additional licences.

## Caveats
- Token classification can be **fact-specific and complex**. Any specific question about whether a particular feature triggers MiFID II rather than MiCA must be escalated to a Vancelian human advisor.
- The regulatory environment for tokenisation is still evolving (delegated acts, ESMA guidelines, member-state interpretations).
- This page reflects Vancelian's stated strategy as of the source documents (August 2025). Specific milestones may evolve.
