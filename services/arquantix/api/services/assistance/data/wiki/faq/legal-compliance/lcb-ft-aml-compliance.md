# How does Vancelian fight money laundering and terrorism financing (AML/CFT)?

> **Regulatory escalation:** the chatbot must escalate any specific compliance, KYC, or sanctions question to a Vancelian human advisor.

## Short answer
Vancelian operates a comprehensive AML/CFT (in French: LCB-FT) dispositif as required by both the French Monetary and Financial Code and EU regulation. The core building blocks are: **KYC** (Know Your Customer) and **KYB** (Know Your Business) verification, **continuous transaction monitoring**, **suspicious activity reporting to TRACFIN** (the French financial intelligence unit), and ongoing staff training. Under the EU **Travel Rule (TFR, Regulation 2023/1113, in force since 30 December 2024)**, Vancelian also transmits originator and beneficiary information with every crypto transfer.

## Details

### Core obligations
- **KYC (Know Your Customer)** — full identity verification of every individual client (ID document, proof of address, dynamic selfie).
- **KYB (Know Your Business)** — equivalent verification of corporate clients and partners.
- **Transaction surveillance** — real-time detection of unusual or suspicious operations (volumes, patterns, counterparties).
- **Suspicious activity reports** — mandatory notifications to **TRACFIN** in case of doubt, in line with French and European obligations.
- **Continuous staff training** on fraud and money-laundering typologies.

### How it works in practice
- KYC must be completed before account features unlock — see [[../account/what-is-kyc-and-why-is-it-necessary|What is KYC and why is it necessary?]].
- Transactions are monitored in real time; preventive freezes can be applied in case of doubt.
- Suspicious files are escalated automatically to the compliance team and, where relevant, to TRACFIN.
- Account suspension or freeze can occur without notice if anomalies are detected (KYC inconsistencies, suspicious operations, CGU breach, judicial request).
- Clients are required to cooperate with any additional information request from the compliance team.

### Travel Rule (TFR) — EU 2023/1113
Since **30 December 2024**, the EU Transfer of Funds Regulation extends the "Travel Rule" to crypto transfers:
- Originator and beneficiary information must accompany every crypto transfer (no minimum threshold, with specific rules above €1,000 for self-hosted wallets).
- Transfers with incomplete or suspicious information can be blocked or flagged for analysis.
- Vancelian has integrated a Travel Rule solution covering VASP-to-VASP transfers, sunrise compliance, and self-hosted wallet handling.

### Sanctions and embargoes
- Vancelian screens clients and counterparties against international sanctions lists.
- Asset freezes are applied where required by law.
- Compliance with EU and French sanctions regimes is mandatory and continuously updated.

### Internal controls
- The AML/CFT dispositif is operated by **dedicated AML/CFT analysts** within the compliance team — see [[vancelian-compliance-team|Vancelian's compliance team]].
- The Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) is the direct interlocutor with the AMF and oversees TRACFIN reporting workflows.
- Regular external audits validate the effectiveness of the dispositif.
- All sensitive operations are documented and retained in line with European requirements.

## Caveats
- KYC, transaction monitoring, and account freezes are **legal obligations** — Vancelian cannot waive them at client request.
- Account holds for AML/CFT reasons may temporarily prevent withdrawals or transactions; the procedure aims to be as fast as possible while meeting regulatory requirements.
- For any account freeze, KYC issue, or compliance file, contact customer support — the chatbot must escalate.
