# I received an email from Vancelian asking about my transactions — is it legitimate?

## Short answer
Yes, it can be legitimate. Our compliance team may contact you directly from `kyc@vancelian.com` to request information about specific transactions, your registration journey, or any element required for the proper monitoring of your account and our regulatory obligations (AML/CFT, KYC). These are routine security and compliance steps. **Always verify the sender address before responding** — and if in doubt, contact `support@vancelian.com` to confirm authenticity.

## Details

### Legitimate compliance contact points
Our teams may contact you:
- **By email** — primarily from `kyc@vancelian.com` for compliance-related requests, or from `support@vancelian.com` for general support.
- **By phone** in some cases.

### What we will NEVER do
- Contact you via **social media** (Instagram, Facebook, WhatsApp, Telegram, TikTok, X, etc.) for compliance or transaction requests.
- Ask for your **payment details** (card number, CVV, full IBAN for extraction).
- Ask for your **login credentials** (password, PIN, 2FA code).

### Why compliance may contact you
Requests typically relate to:
- Specific transactions requiring additional context (source of funds, purpose, counterparty)
- Your registration journey (missing or unclear documents)
- Any element needed for ongoing compliance monitoring under AML/CFT regulation

## Process — what to do when you receive such an email
1. **Check the sender address carefully** — legitimate compliance emails come from `kyc@vancelian.com`. Look for subtle misspellings (e.g. `vancelian-support.com`, `vanceliam.com`).
2. **Do not click on suspicious links**. If in doubt, open the Vancelian app directly rather than following a link.
3. **Verify if unsure** — forward the email to `support@vancelian.com` and ask for confirmation before responding.
4. **Respond only after verification** with the information requested, attaching documents if applicable.

## Caveats
- Phishing attempts imitating Vancelian exist. Our rule of thumb: **when in doubt, ask support before replying**.
- We never ask for credentials or payment card details by email or phone.
- See also [[social-media-security-awareness]] for broader security guidance.
