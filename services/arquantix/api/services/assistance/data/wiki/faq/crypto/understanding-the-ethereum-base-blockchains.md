# Understanding the Ethereum & Base Blockchains

## Short answer
Ethereum is the most established and secure blockchain but has higher transaction fees. Base is a Layer 2 blockchain built on Ethereum that provides faster and significantly cheaper transactions while maintaining Ethereum's security through rollup technology.

## Details
#### **Why do we offer Ethereum and Base as blockchains on our platform?**
At Vancelian, we chose to integrate Ethereum and Base to give you the best balance of **security, accessibility, and performance.**
These two blockchains are complementary and built on the same underlying technology, but they deliver different experiences in terms of cost and speed.
 

#### **What is the Ethereum blockchain?**

- Ethereum is one of the **most established** and **secure** blockchains in the world.

- It hosts a wide range of decentralised applications (DeFi, NFTs, etc.).

- Every transaction on Ethereum is processed and validated individually by the network, ensuring a high level of **security.** However, this also means that transaction fees can be quite high, especially during periods of heavy network activity.

**What is the Base blockchain?**

- Base is a Layer 2 blockchain built on top of Ethereum.

- It was developed by Coinbase to improve access to blockchain applications.

- Base enables **faster** and **cheaper** transactions, while still relying on Ethereum’s **security.**

- It uses a technology called rollup, which bundles multiple transactions together and records them all at once on Ethereum. This is the key factor that significantly reduces transaction costs.

In summary: with Base, you benefit from the same level of security, but with faster execution and significantly lower fees.
 

#### **Do Ethereum and Base use the same standard?**
Base relies entirely on Ethereum for security and final validation. This means that even if your transactions are first processed by Base, they are ultimately anchored on Ethereum in a verifiable and immutable way.

**This implies that:**

- The same rules apply on both networks

- Tokens behave the same way (transfer, signature, etc.)

- Your Ethereum wallets are also valid on Base
 

#### **Key takeaways**

- Ethereum is the reference blockchain: highly secure but costly.

- Base is a modern, fast, and cost-effective solution, perfectly suited for everyday use.

- Your funds and transactions are just as secure on Base as they are on Ethereum.
