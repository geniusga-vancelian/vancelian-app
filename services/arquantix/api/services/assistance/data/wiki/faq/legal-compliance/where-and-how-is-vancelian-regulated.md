# Where and how is Vancelian regulated?

## Short answer
Vancelian is operated by Automata France SAS (registered with the AMF as PSAN E2023-087) and has an equivalent PSAN registration in Italy. It is currently applying for a PSCA licence under MiCA for full EU/EEA passporting. In the UAE, Automata FZE has received VARA In-Principle Approval (IPA) with full licence expected imminently.
## Details
**Automata ICO Limited** is an Irish company with its registered office at 3rd Floor Ormond Building, 31-36 Ormond Quay Upper, Dublin 7, D07 Ee37, Ireland, registered in Ireland under number 690280 (“Automata ICO Limited”).

**Automata ICO Limited** has a branch in Italy, with its registered office located at Via Archimede, 161, Rome, Italy. The company is registered with the Organismo degli Agenti e dei Mediatori (“AOM”) under number 96550860587 as a Digital Asset Service Provider (“PSAN”).

**Automata France SAS** is a French company registered under SIREN number 902 498 617. The company is registered with the Autorité des Marchés Financiers (“AMF”) under number E2023-087 as a Digital Asset Service Provider (“PSAN”).

**Automata France SAS** is an outsourced service provider of Modulr Finance B.V., a company registered in the Netherlands under business number 81852401, which is authorised and regulated by the Dutch Central Bank (DNB) as an Electronic Money Institution (company reference number: R182870) for the issuance of electronic money and payment services.

**Vancelian is currently pursuing its regulatory development internationally.**

**In Europe**, Automata France SAS has submitted a **PSCA (Prestataire de Services sur Crypto-Actifs)** licence application to the AMF under **MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation)**. The PSCA authorisation, once granted, will enable Vancelian to passport its services across all EU/EEA member states.

The application covers **7 crypto-asset services** as defined by MiCA:

1. **Custody and administration of crypto-assets** (Art. 75) — Automata France SAS as PSAN/PSCA depositary, operating segregated per-client wallets secured via the Fireblocks MPC key-management infrastructure (full architecture in [[custody-architecture]])
2. **Exchange of crypto-assets for funds** (Art. 77) — EUR ↔ crypto trading on the Vancelian app
3. **Exchange of crypto-assets for other crypto-assets** (Art. 77) — crypto-to-crypto swaps
4. **Execution of orders on behalf of clients** (Art. 78) — own-account interposition model (Automata France is sole counterparty)
5. **Reception and transmission of orders** (Art. 79) — order routing and processing
6. **Providing advice on crypto-assets** (Art. 81) — limited to informational and educational content
7. **Portfolio management of crypto-assets** (Art. 80) — Multi-Digital Assets service (Crypto Baskets) with automatic rebalancing

> **Status (as of April 2026):** the PSCA application is under review by the AMF. Automata France continues to operate under its existing PSAN E2023-087 registration during the transition period, which ends on **1 July 2026** — a hard deadline confirmed by the AMF (February 2026), with no extension possible. See [What happens if Vancelian does not obtain MiCA](what-happens-if-vancelian-does-not-obtain-mica.md) for more.

**In the United Arab Emirates**, **Automata FZE** (Dubai) has obtained **In-Principle Approval (IPA)** from the **VARA** (Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority) in December 2025. The full VARA licence is expected imminently and covers: Broker Dealer, Management & Investments, Lending & Borrowing, and Advisory services. Once granted, the Vancelian Dubai app will launch, and the group will be among the first VARA-regulated players with a consumer-facing app.

> **Note:** this page will be updated as soon as the full VARA licence is officially announced.

**Current operational registrations** — France (PSAN E2023-087, AMF) and Italy (AOM 96550860587, via Automata ICO Limited) — allow Vancelian to serve clients in those jurisdictions directly. European citizens outside France and Italy may access Vancelian services under the **reverse solicitation** principle (the client approaches Vancelian on their own initiative). Once the MiCA/PSCA authorisation is granted, Vancelian will be able to actively market and serve clients across all EU/EEA member states.

These steps reflect Vancelian's commitment to offering a compliant, secure environment aligned with the highest regulatory standards.

For a detailed breakdown of all 7 PSCA services and their MiCA article references, see [Vancelian MiCA services overview](vancelian-mica-services-overview.md).
