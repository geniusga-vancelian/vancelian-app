# Guarantees and security of your investment — Dubai Villa Al Barari

## Short answer
The question is not one of guarantee but of the engagement mechanic on your capital. Dubai Villa Al Barari is a **100% crypto loan** to **Solaria**, the SPV / project counterparty, which acquires and renovates the villa. Solaria has a **contractual obligation to repay the capital and interest** according to the offer's schedule. Specific protections (pre-financing, cash-flow margin, de facto collateral via the property, partner credentials) are detailed in the commercial brochure and offer documentation in the app. **Absolute limit: no protection, whatever its form, covers a total or partial default of Solaria as the counterparty.** Returns are indicative and not guaranteed.

## Details

#### Engagement mechanic and absolute limit

The investment takes the form of a 100% crypto loan from the investor to Solaria, which finances the acquisition and renovation of the villa. Solaria has a contractual obligation to repay the capital and interest according to the offer's schedule. **No protection, whatever its form, covers a total or partial default of Solaria as the counterparty.** This is the absolute limit of any structured mitigation and it cannot be softened by listing safeguards.

Within this limit, the project documentation describes several layers of structured risk mitigation:

#### 1. Pre-financing (refinancing model)

This is a refinancing operation: Solaria has already committed an initial contribution to the project before the collection opens to investors. This means the project is not starting from zero — it is already underway, and investor funds complement an existing commitment. This also enables the payment of interest from the very first day of your deposit.

#### 2. Cash flow margin in the business plan

The financial plan includes a built-in margin to absorb potential cost overruns or delays. This buffer is designed to ensure that the terms of the offer — both the daily returns and the capital repayment at maturity — can be honoured even if the project encounters unforeseen expenses.

#### 3. Premium location strategy

The villa is located in The Nest, Al Barari — one of Dubai's most exclusive residential developments. The choice of a premium, high-demand location is deliberate: it maximises the likelihood of a successful sale at or above the projected price, and reduces the risk of an extended sale period.

#### 4. Controlled execution

Solaria works with established local partners: Al Hilal Engineering Consultants for construction and architecture, and Maria Morris Global for real estate brokerage. The acquisition is made below market value, the renovation follows a controlled scope, and the resale is prepared through 3D pre-listing with leading brokers — all designed to secure the business plan.

#### 5. The property as de facto collateral

Solaria is contractually bound to repay the capital and interest. In all scenarios, Solaria holds the property as a tangible asset backing the loan. Even in the unlikely event of a delayed sale, the property remains — providing a de facto security for the invested capital.

#### The residual risk

The primary residual risk is the inability to sell the property at the expected price or within the expected timeframe, which could delay capital repayment. This counterparty risk on Solaria cannot be eliminated entirely by any structured mitigation.

#### Separation of roles

Solaria (via a dedicated SPV for each project) solely bears the debt, the risk, and the obligation to repay capital and interest. Vancelian (Automata France SAS), as a DASP registered with the AMF, provides the regulated platform and digital asset custody — Vancelian does not guarantee repayment.

#### Where to find the full details

The commercial brochure, business plan details, partner credentials, and specific offer conditions are available directly in the offer detail on the Vancelian app. The brochure is also available on the Vancelian website. These documents provide the in-depth information needed to assess the project before investing.

Returns are indicative and not guaranteed. The investment carries a risk of capital loss.
