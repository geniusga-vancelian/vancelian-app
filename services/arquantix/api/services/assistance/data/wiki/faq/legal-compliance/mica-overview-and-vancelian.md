# What is MiCA and why is it important for Vancelian?

> **Regulatory escalation:** the chatbot must escalate any specific regulatory or legal question to a Vancelian human advisor. The content below is informational only and does not constitute legal advice.

## Short answer
**MiCA** stands for **Markets in Crypto-Assets** — the European Union regulation (Regulation (EU) 2023/1114) that harmonises the rules for crypto-asset issuance and crypto-asset service providers across all 27 EU member states. Adopted in 2023 with progressive application through 2026, it brings crypto into a single, investor-protective framework. Vancelian, currently registered as a **PSAN** (Prestataire de Services sur Actifs Numériques) with the French AMF, is preparing its transition to the new **CASP** (Crypto-Asset Service Provider) status under MiCA — a key step in its European growth and institutionalisation strategy.

## Details

### MiCA's four core objectives
MiCA was built around four objectives that the European legislator wanted to achieve simultaneously. It aims first to **harmonise the rules** across the 27 EU member states and eliminate "regulatory shopping" — until MiCA, each country set its own framework, which created uneven protection and distorted competition. Second, it aims to **protect investors** through a unified, strengthened legal framework that applies equally everywhere. Third, it aims to **increase transparency** with standardised disclosure on risks, fees and service conditions, so that a client reading documentation in Paris sees the same structure as a client reading documentation in Frankfurt. Fourth, it aims to **strengthen financial stability**, notably through stricter rules on stablecoins — specifically asset-referenced tokens (ART) and e-money tokens (EMT) — which had been identified as a potential source of systemic risk.

### Who MiCA applies to — and who it excludes
MiCA applies to **issuers** that offer crypto-assets to the public or seek admission to trading, and to **Crypto-Asset Service Providers (CASPs)** — custodians, exchanges, trading platforms, advisors, portfolio managers and crypto-transfer providers. Several categories sit explicitly outside MiCA: crypto-assets that already qualify as **financial instruments under MiFID II** (they remain under traditional financial law), **genuinely unique non-fungible NFTs**, and **banking deposits, insurance products and pension schemes** (covered by their own frameworks). Mining and cloud mining as standalone activities are also excluded — no MiCA licence is required for selling computing power, which is why Vancelian's Cloud Mining offer does not need a MiCA CASP licence for that activity specifically (see [[../exclusive-offers/cloud-mining-mica-and-european-regulation|Cloud Mining and European regulation]]).

### Application timeline — and why 1 July 2026 is a hard deadline
MiCA entered into force in phases. The first milestone was **30 June 2024**, when the ART and EMT provisions became applicable — these are the stablecoin rules. On **30 December 2024**, the rest of the regulation applied, including the CASP obligations. The final milestone — and the one that matters most operationally for Vancelian — is **1 July 2026**, the end of the 18-month national grandfathering window that ran from 30 December 2024. After that date, only providers **authorised as CASP under MiCA** may continue providing crypto-asset services in France. PSANs that have not obtained the authorisation must cease activity, because continuing without it exposes them to criminal penalties — up to **2 years of imprisonment and a €30,000 fine** under Articles L. 54-10-4 and L. 572-23 of the French Monetary and Financial Code. PSANs not pursuing CASP authorisation must start an orderly wind-down plan **no later than 30 March 2026** per the AMF's February 2026 notice. **There is no extension beyond 1 July 2026** — this is the regulatory reality that shapes Vancelian's roadmap.

### Why Vancelian needs MiCA
Vancelian operates today as **Automata France SAS**, PSAN-registered with the French AMF (SIREN 902 498 617, AMF registration E2023-087). From 2026 onwards, every PSAN must be **CASP-compliant** to continue operating legally in Europe, which means obtaining MiCA authorisation is not optional — it is the condition for continued activity. Beyond that regulatory necessity, the authorisation delivers three concrete benefits for the business: it **strengthens trust** with retail and institutional investors thanks to a recognised compliance framework, it allows Vancelian to **distribute services across the entire European Union** under a single passport (no more patchwork of national registrations), and it brings **all the Vancelian products — Cloud Mining distribution, Vaults, Exclusive Offers, AKTIO/VNC tokens — into explicit alignment** with the new requirements on AML/CFT, governance, risk control, and operational and financial security.

### What clients gain from MiCA
From the client perspective, MiCA changes the nature of the relationship with a crypto platform. It introduces **enhanced security** through strict obligations on client fund protection, asset segregation, minimum capital requirements and regular audits. It imposes **standardised transparency** — a validated whitepaper, a simplified prospectus, and clear information on fees, risks and contractual conditions — so that the client sees the same documentation structure regardless of the provider. It gives **trust** through official recognition of Vancelian as a regulated European actor with the right to communicate its offers freely and legally across the EU. And it delivers **broader access**: once CASP-authorised, Vancelian can commercially solicit any EU citizen, which opens the full European market to clients looking for its regulated products.

## Caveats
- MiCA is **not** a guarantee against investment loss. It is a framework that improves transparency, governance, and consumer protection — the underlying market and operational risks of crypto products remain.
