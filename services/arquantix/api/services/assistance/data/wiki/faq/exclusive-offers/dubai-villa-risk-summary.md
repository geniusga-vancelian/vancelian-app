# What are the risks of the Dubai Villa — Al Barari offer?

## Short answer
The Dubai Villa — Al Barari offer carries **no formal capital guarantee**. Your exposure is to a single real-estate renovation project operated by **Solaria**, the project counterparty, with an engagement running until **1 May 2027**. The indicative return of **10.7% to 11.5% per year** (depending on your Privilege Club status) reflects this direct-lending model: fewer intermediaries, higher rate for you — in exchange for accepting counterparty risk on Solaria and the project itself. Solaria has structured its business plan with a **margin designed to absorb a measured level of adverse variance** (costs, timing, sale price), which mitigates — but does not eliminate — the risk.

## Details

### The four families of risk

**1. Project execution risk**
Construction delays, cost overruns, or quality issues on the renovation works. Mitigated by the fact that Solaria pre-finances the project, has built-in cash-flow margin in its business plan, and operates under controlled execution.

**2. Real-estate market risk**
The resale value of the villa at the end of the project depends on the Dubai luxury real-estate market. A market downturn between now and the sale could reduce the exit proceeds. Mitigated by a premium location (Al Barari) and by Solaria's underwritten margin on the business plan.

**3. Counterparty risk — Solaria**
Solaria is the project counterparty. It carries the debt and the operational responsibility. Vancelian acts as the platform connecting the client with the programme; it is not the economic counterparty. If Solaria defaulted (insolvency, force majeure, operational failure), the practical ability to honour the scheduled repayment would be affected. This risk is **not eliminated** — it is the main risk on this offer.

**4. Liquidity / timing risk**
Committed capital is locked until **1 May 2027**. Early exit is possible only during the **semi-annual exit windows** (May and November), and only if an incoming investor takes over your position. Partial exits are possible. Early exit fees apply before 24 months of holding.

### How the 10.7%–11.5% rate reflects the risk
The rate is not a yield promise — it is the **direct reflection of the lending model**. By lending directly to the project counterparty, you capture the margin that banking intermediation would otherwise take. You are compensated for the counterparty risk you accept on Solaria and the project. A higher rate does not mean a higher guarantee; it means a more concentrated exposure.

### What Solaria's margin covers
Solaria has built its business plan with a margin intended to absorb **measured adverse variance**: cost overruns, execution delays, or a sale price moderately below the central case. This margin is **not infinite** — an extreme scenario (severe market downturn, operational failure of the counterparty) would exceed what the margin can absorb and would materially affect capital recovery.

### Where to find the full details
The commercial brochure and the specific offer conditions — business plan assumptions, counterparty profile, partner information, timeline, and the precise contractual framework — are available directly in the Vancelian app in the **detail of the offer**, and on the Vancelian website. These resources answer detailed due-diligence questions more precisely than this summary.

## Caveats
- There is **no formal capital guarantee** and no deposit-guarantee scheme applicable to this investment.
- Returns are indicative, paid in EURC, and **not guaranteed**. Past performance does not predict future results.
- Committed capital is locked until 1 May 2027; early exit is conditional on an incoming investor.
- Specific contractual mechanisms in an adverse scenario (waterfall, allocation of residual proceeds, priorities between Solaria and investors) are detailed in the offer documentation rather than summarised here. For any question on a specific contractual clause or a specific scenario, escalate to the Vancelian support team at `support@vancelian.com`.
