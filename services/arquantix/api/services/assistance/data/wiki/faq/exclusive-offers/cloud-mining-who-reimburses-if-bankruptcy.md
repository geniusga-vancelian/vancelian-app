# Cloud Mining: who is responsible for reimbursement in case of bankruptcy?

## Short answer
Cloud Mining is a **computing-power purchase contract**, not a loan. Your contractual counterparty is **Vancelian LTD** (UAE-ADGM), a joint venture between Automata Group LTD (UK) and Hearst Solution FZCO. Under **article 4.3 of the CGUPM**, Vancelian LTD is contractually committed to reimburse your capital at maturity. In a bankruptcy scenario, that commitment is **bounded by the own funds and financial capacity of Vancelian LTD** — if insufficient, a partial or total loss of capital is possible. Refer to the CGUPM or Vancelian support for specifics.

## Details

### How the Cloud Mining mechanic works
Cloud Mining is **not a loan**. When you subscribe, you purchase a share of computing power operated by the mining infrastructure. In return, you receive daily rewards generated by Bitcoin mining activity. Vancelian acts as the **intermediary** between you (the client) and the entity that produces the yield.

The contractual counterparty for this offer is **Vancelian LTD**, registered in Abu Dhabi (ADGM). Vancelian LTD is a joint venture co-managed by Automata Group LTD (UK) and Hearst Solution FZCO. Hearst Solution FZCO is the **technical operator** of the mining infrastructure — it manages the hardware, the sites, and the energy supply. Vancelian LTD is the entity that holds the contractual obligation towards you.

### What happens at maturity
Under article 4.3 of the CGUPM (Conditions Générales d'Utilisation du Programme de Minage), Vancelian LTD undertakes to reimburse your invested capital at the end of the engagement period. At that point, the capital is unlocked and made available for withdrawal in your Vancelian wallet.

### What happens in a bankruptcy or default scenario
The contractual reimbursement commitment is only as solid as the financial capacity of the counterparty. In a default scenario:

- **If Vancelian LTD has sufficient own funds** to cover its obligations, the reimbursement proceeds as contractually agreed.
- **If Vancelian LTD defaults without sufficient own funds**, a partial or total loss of capital is possible.
- **If the mining output is insufficient** to sustain the programme economics over the contract period, a remediation plan may be implemented — but this remains subject to the counterparty's capacity.

This is a **counterparty risk** inherent to the structure of the offer. There is no external deposit guarantee, no state-backed protection scheme, and no third-party guarantee beyond the own funds and assets of Vancelian LTD.

### Important distinctions
- Cloud Mining is a **computing power purchase contract** — it is not a loan, not a deposit, and not a regulated savings product.
- **Vancelian (Automata France SAS)** acts as intermediary: it provides the exchange, custody, and app display services. It does not operate the mining infrastructure and is not the contractual counterparty for the reimbursement.
- **Hearst Solution FZCO** is the technical operator of the mining sites — not the direct contractual counterparty of your investment.

## Caveats
- The information above describes the general contractual framework. Specific legal interpretation depends on your individual contract terms and the applicable CGUPM version.
- For any question about a specific default scenario, your contractual rights, or recovery procedures, **contact Vancelian support at support@vancelian.com** or consult the official offer documentation and T&Cs directly in the Vancelian app.
- The chatbot cannot provide legal advice on insolvency proceedings or contractual interpretation — these questions must be escalated to a human advisor.
