# How does the Bitcoin halving affect Cloud Mining returns?

## Short answer
Every ~4 years (every 210,000 blocks), Bitcoin's protocol cuts the per-block reward in half — the **halving**. The immediate effect is a 50% drop in BTC mined per unit of hashrate, mechanically compressing yields. Historically, halvings have been followed by Bitcoin bull markets driven by scarcity, which can offset the yield drop — but this is **not guaranteed**. Vancelian and Hearst manage halving exposure by sourcing efficient machines, optimising energy costs, and retaining a portion of mined BTC as a long-term stabilising reserve.

## Details

### What is the halving?
The halving is a hard-coded event in the Bitcoin protocol: every **210,000 blocks** (~4 years), the block subsidy paid to miners is cut in half. This caps Bitcoin's total supply at **21 million BTC** and enforces programmed scarcity.

### Direct impact on mining yield
After a halving, miners receive **half as many BTC** for the same computing power. If the BTC price doesn't rise, the yield drops mechanically. Operating costs (energy, machines, logistics) stay the same, so the **break-even threshold rises** and only the most efficient operators stay competitive. Less competitive miners (older machines, high-cost sites) often shut down, temporarily reducing total network hashrate before the next difficulty re-target.

### Why halvings have historically supported Bitcoin's price
Each halving reduces the rate of new BTC issuance. With supply growth slowing and demand stable or rising, history shows a typical pattern:
1. **Bull market** in the months following the halving.
2. **Correction (bear market)** later in the cycle.

These ~4-year cycles structure mining economics. They are **not guaranteed** to repeat, but they are the dominant pattern observed across the 2012, 2016, 2020 (and 2024) halvings.

### How Vancelian stabilises yield through halvings
Two main levers:
1. **Hardware sourcing** — buying performant Bitmain machines at the best market price to maximise efficiency per watt.
2. **BTC reserve strategy** — retaining a portion of mined BTC. As Bitcoin's structural scarcity drives long-term price appreciation, the reserve helps stabilise yield even when the per-block reward halves.

### What an investor should remember
Cloud Mining returns must be evaluated on a **long horizon** that integrates these natural cycles. A short-term yield drop after a halving is normal; the relevant question is the multi-year average across a full cycle.

## Caveats
- Halving cycles are a historical pattern, **not a guarantee**.
- Cloud Mining is not for short-term traders — capital is locked until contract maturity (31 December 2028 for all new and migrated positions) precisely because the product makes sense across cycles, not within a single quarter.
- The Cloud Mining offer is **not available indefinitely**. As the next halving approaches and difficulty rises, Vancelian may not renew the offer if yields no longer make sense.
