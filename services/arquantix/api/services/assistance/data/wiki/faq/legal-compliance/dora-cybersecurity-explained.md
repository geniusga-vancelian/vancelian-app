# What is DORA and how does Vancelian comply with it?

> **Regulatory escalation:** the chatbot must escalate any specific cybersecurity or operational-resilience question to a Vancelian human advisor.

## Short answer
**DORA** = **Digital Operational Resilience Act** (EU Regulation 2022/2554), in force since **17 January 2025**. It applies to financial actors — including crypto-asset service providers — and requires strong cybersecurity, business continuity, incident reporting, and regular security testing. DORA complements MiCA by covering the **technical and operational dimension**, ensuring Vancelian's platform stays available and secure even under cyber-attack or major incident. Vancelian has built a critical-function map, business continuity plans (BCP/DRP), independent ICT audits, intrusion testing, and a reinforced logging and traceability stack.

## Details

### What DORA requires from Vancelian
- **Cybersecurity policies** — strict standards for ICT risk management.
- **Business continuity & disaster recovery (BCP/DRP)** — regularly tested plans.
- **Incident reporting** — mandatory notification to authorities for major ICT incidents.
- **Regular security testing** — stress tests, audits, threat-led penetration testing (TLPT).
- **Third-party ICT provider oversight** — due diligence, contracts with DORA clauses, contingency planning.

### How Vancelian implements DORA
- **Critical function and ICT mapping** — including external providers and dependencies.
- **Business continuity plans (BCP/PRA) regularly tested** to validate recovery in disruptive scenarios.
- **Independent ICT audits and intrusion tests** — performed by external specialists.
- **Reinforced logging and traceability** — immutable journals to support forensic analysis.
- **SOC / EDR** — security operations centre and endpoint detection & response capability.
- **Annual DORA report** to management and regulators.

### How DORA fits with MiCA and the broader regulatory stack
DORA is one element of a broader European compliance framework that protects clients on multiple dimensions:
- **MiCA** — frames crypto services and brings transparency and governance.
- **DORA** — strengthens IT resilience and cybersecurity.
- **AML/CFT (LCB-FT)** — protects against criminal misuse of crypto-assets.
- **GDPR** — protects user personal data.
- **MiFID II / Prospectus** — frames tokens that qualify as financial instruments.
- **TFR (Travel Rule)** — ensures traceability of crypto transfers (EU 2023/1113).

Together these regulations create a layered legal, technical, and financial protection for Vancelian clients.

## Caveats
- DORA reduces ICT risk but does not eliminate it. No regulation can guarantee zero incidents — only well-managed response capability.
- For details on a specific cyber incident or service outage, contact Vancelian customer support.
