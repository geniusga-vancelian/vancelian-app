# Vancelian's regulatory status in the UAE: VARA In-Principle Approval

## Short answer
**Vancelian (Automata FZE) has received an In-Principle Approval (IPA) from Dubai's Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (VARA)** for four regulated activity categories: **Broker Dealer, Management & Investments, Lending & Borrowing, and Advisory**. This is an important regulatory milestone but **not a full licence** — Vancelian must still obtain final authorisation from VARA before any regulated virtual asset activities can start in the UAE.

## Details

### What is VARA?
VARA (Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority) is the dedicated virtual assets regulator for the Emirate of Dubai, established in 2022. Any company providing virtual asset services in Dubai must be licensed by VARA.

### What VARA approved
Vancelian's UAE entity, **Automata FZE**, has been granted In-Principle Approval (IPA) for the following regulated activity categories:

- **Broker Dealer** — facilitating virtual asset trades on behalf of clients
- **Management & Investments** — managing virtual asset portfolios and investment products
- **Lending & Borrowing** — virtual-asset-based credit operations
- **Advisory** — providing investment advice on virtual assets

### What an IPA means (and does not mean)
- **An IPA is a stage in the VARA licensing process**, not a full licence.
- It signals that VARA considers Vancelian's governance framework, long-term strategy and compliance posture to meet the bar for the next stage of review.
- **Regulated virtual asset activities in the UAE may only start once Vancelian has received the final authorisation from VARA** — not before.
- Vancelian was supported through the process by **W3C**, its regulatory advisors.

### What this changes today
- **Nothing operationally for current Vancelian clients in the EU**, who continue to be served by Vancelian's French entity Automata France SAS, registered as a **DASP with the AMF under number E2023-087**.
- This is preparatory groundwork for future regulated activity in Dubai under the Automata FZE entity.

## Caveats
- The In-Principle Approval **does not constitute a full licence**. Vancelian cannot legally start regulated VA activities in the UAE until VARA grants final authorisation.
- IPA timelines for full licensing vary by applicant — Vancelian has not publicly committed to a date.
- Clients should not interpret the IPA as a guarantee of any specific service launch date in the UAE.
