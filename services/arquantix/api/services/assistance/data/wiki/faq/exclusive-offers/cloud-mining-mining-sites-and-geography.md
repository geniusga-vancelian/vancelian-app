# Where are Vancelian's Cloud Mining sites located?

## Short answer
Vancelian's Cloud Mining capacity is operated by [[../entities/hearst|Hearst]], which runs sites across **7 countries** (USA, Brazil, Ethiopia, Oman, Kazakhstan, Norway, UAE). Vancelian's specific deployment — approximately **€40 million of hardware** — is concentrated on three sites: **Ethiopia, the United States, and Kazakhstan**. Geographic diversification reduces single-location risk (energy, regulation, climate).

## Details

### Why diversify across multiple countries
A single-location strategy concentrates several risks: energy instability, regulatory shifts, climate events, and local political risk. Spreading capacity across three jurisdictions improves the resilience of the overall programme.

### Ethiopia
Ethiopia gives Hearst privileged access to abundant, competitive **hydroelectric** power (notably the Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam). Low-cost, low-carbon electricity supports both margin and sustainability. The flagship site at **Legetafo** spans 20,000 m² with a 20 MW capacity, equipped with Bitmain S21 Pro+ machines.

### United States
The US offers a mature technological ecosystem, regulatory stability in selected states, and access to diversified energy infrastructure (renewables, grid, gas recovery). Strong legal certainty balances operational efficiency.

### Kazakhstan
Kazakhstan is a major Central Asian mining hub. It combines competitive electricity prices with favourable conditions for hosting large-scale machine fleets — strong price/performance for high-density deployments.

### Cooling strategy by site
Each site uses the cooling technology best suited to local climate and density:
- **Air cooling** — fan-based, suited to moderate climates.
- **Hydro (liquid) cooling** — circulating liquid absorbs heat efficiently in hot climates and high-density deployments.

### Hearst's sustainability approach
Where possible, Hearst prioritises low-impact energy: **hydro** (Ethiopia, Norway), **flared gas recovery**, and grid **load balancing** (the "mining as a battery" concept — absorbing surplus energy and helping stabilise local grids).

## Caveats
- Vancelian's deployment is currently focused on Ethiopia/USA/Kazakhstan, but this may evolve as Hearst expands its global network.
- Each jurisdiction carries specific regulatory risk; see [[cloud-mining-risks-overview|Cloud Mining risks overview]].
