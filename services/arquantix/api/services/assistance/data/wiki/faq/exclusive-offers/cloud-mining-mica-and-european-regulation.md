# Is Cloud Mining regulated in Europe? How does MiCA apply?

## Short answer
Cloud Mining itself is **not** covered by a dedicated European licence. The EU's **MiCA** regulation (in force from 2024–2025) frames crypto-asset services (custody, exchange, advisory, management) but **explicitly excludes mining as an activity**. Vancelian's Cloud Mining offer — which provides hashrate via [[../entities/hearst|Hearst]] without packaging it as a guaranteed financial product — therefore falls **outside MiCA's scope**. The applicable rules concern consumer protection (clear contracts), taxation in the investor's country of residence, and indirect AML obligations on the operator. **Regulatory questions should always be escalated to a Vancelian human advisor.**

## Details

### What MiCA actually covers
MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation) governs:
- Custody of crypto-assets
- Buying and selling on behalf of clients
- Advisory and portfolio management
- Issuance of asset-referenced and e-money tokens

**Mining is not in this list.** A pure Cloud Mining operator who sells hashrate (not a structured investment product) is not a MiCA-regulated activity.

### When mining could become a regulated financial product
If a Cloud Mining offer were structured as a **fixed guaranteed return** or as a **pooled investment vehicle**, it could be reclassified as a financial product and require authorisation from the relevant national regulator (e.g. AMF in France, BaFin in Germany). Vancelian's offer is **not** structured this way — the yield is variable and explicitly tied to mining activity output, which keeps it outside the financial-product definition.

### What rules **do** apply to Cloud Mining in Europe
1. **Consumer protection law** — contracts must be clear, accurate, and not misleading. Vancelian's CGUPM (Cloud Mining T&Cs) document the variable nature of the yield, fees, exit conditions, and risk disclosures.
2. **Taxation** — mining rewards are taxable in the investor's **country of residence**. Vancelian does not provide personalised tax advice; investors are responsible for declaring their gains under local rules.
3. **Anti-money-laundering (AML / KYC)** — when an operator holds, converts, or resells mined crypto, AML obligations apply at the conversion/resale step. Vancelian operates standard KYC on all clients.

### Vancelian's regulatory status
Vancelian operates through Automata France as the European distribution intermediary, holds regulated status in France (DASP / PSAN registration), and is supervised by the French AMF for its crypto-asset services. Cloud Mining hashrate distribution sits alongside — but distinct from — those regulated services.

## Caveats
- **The chatbot must escalate any regulatory or tax question to a Vancelian human advisor.** Regulation is jurisdiction-specific and evolving — generic answers are not sufficient.
- Tax treatment varies by country. Always verify with a local tax professional.
- MiCA's interaction with mining may evolve in future EU rulemaking; this page reflects the situation as of August 2025.
