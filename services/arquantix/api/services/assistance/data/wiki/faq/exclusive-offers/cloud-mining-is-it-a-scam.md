# Is Cloud Mining a scam? How to recognise a legitimate operator

## Short answer
Legitimate Cloud Mining is built on **real, measurable computing power** operated in physical data centres. Scam operators are easy to spot: they promise **fixed guaranteed returns**, hide the identity of the infrastructure operator, give no detail on machines or sites, and have no working customer support. A serious operator clearly explains its costs, risks, and limits — and **never guarantees a yield**.

## Details

### Red flags of a Cloud Mining scam
- **Guaranteed fixed return.** No real mining operation can promise a fixed yield because it depends on Bitcoin price, network difficulty, and energy costs — all of which move constantly.
- **Opaque infrastructure.** No information on machines, no site locations, no operator name.
- **No real customer support.** No way to reach a human, no documented complaint procedure.
- **No T&Cs or contract.** No legal documentation framing the relationship.
- **Pressure to recruit others.** A multi-level recruitment incentive structure is a strong fraud signal.

### What a legitimate operator does
- **Discloses risks transparently** — including the possibility of capital loss.
- **Names the infrastructure operator** — Vancelian's partner is [[../entities/hearst|Hearst]], with a documented track record (10,000+ machines, 2+ EH/s, 7 countries, 500+ BTC mined in 2025).
- **Documents the machines and sites** — Vancelian's deployment uses Bitmain Antminer S21 Pro+ across Ethiopia, the United States, and Kazakhstan.
- **Publishes T&Cs** — see Vancelian's CGUPM (Conditions Générales d'Utilisation – Programme de Mining), accessible from your client area.
- **Provides exit conditions** — Vancelian's offer has documented early-exit windows (May, November) and a defined fee schedule.
- **Operates real customer support** — accessible via email and the Vancelian app.

### Why Vancelian's offer is structured this way
Vancelian (via Automata France) acts as a distribution intermediary for European clients. It is responsible for the proper management of contracts and the transparency of information — but it cannot and does not guarantee a target rate. This is a deliberate choice that reflects the reality of mining economics, not a limitation of the offer.

## Caveats
- Even a legitimate operator carries risk — see [[cloud-mining-risks-overview|Cloud Mining risks overview]] and [[cloud-mining-can-i-lose-my-capital|Can I lose my capital?]].
- If anything in another platform's offer feels "too good to be true", it almost certainly is.
