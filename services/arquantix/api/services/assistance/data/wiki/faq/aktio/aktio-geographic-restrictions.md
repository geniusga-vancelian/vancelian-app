# Geographic restrictions for AKTIO

## Short answer
At the time of the Initial Offering (2021–2022), **AKTIO was not available to citizens, tax residents or green card holders** of the **United States, China, Hong Kong and Malta**. These exclusions were enforced through KYC/AML procedures and embedded in the AKTIO smart contract. For current purchases via Vancelian, geographic eligibility follows **Vancelian's own availability rules** and the policies of the secondary exchanges where AKTIO is listed.

## Details

### ICO restrictions (April 2022 whitepaper)
During the Initial AKTIO Coin Offering, buyers were **not eligible** to purchase AKTIO if they were citizens, residents (for tax or otherwise) or green card holders of:

- **United States of America**
- **People's Republic of China**
- **Hong Kong Special Administrative Region**
- **Malta**

Two mechanisms enforced these restrictions:

- **KYC / buyer due diligence** procedures screened out ineligible persons at onboarding.
- The **AKTIO smart contract** provides that a purchase by an ineligible person is **void and unenforceable** against Akt.io or any third party.

Automata ICO Ltd reserved the right to update the restricted-country list at its discretion, so additional high-risk jurisdictions may also apply.

### Secondary market and Vancelian app (current)
Post-ICO, AKTIO is also available on secondary crypto-asset exchanges such as **BitMart** and **Bittrex Global**. Availability on those venues follows the policies of each exchange, not Automata ICO.

Within the **Vancelian application**, eligibility to open an account, hold or trade AKTIO follows Vancelian's own availability rules — see [[where-is-the-vancelian-application-available]] for the current list of supported countries, and [[us-persons-and-use-of-the-vancelian-app]] for the specific treatment of US persons.

## Caveats
- These restrictions reflect rules **as enforced during the ICO** in 2021–2022. For any current acquisition of AKTIO, please confirm eligibility with Vancelian support.
- US-person status extends beyond citizenship to include residents and green card holders for tax purposes.
