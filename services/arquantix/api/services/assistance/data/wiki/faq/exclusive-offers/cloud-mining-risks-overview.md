# Cloud Mining: what are the risks?

## Short answer
Cloud Mining is a **computing power purchase contract** — not a loan. Returns are **variable and never guaranteed**. The main risks are: Bitcoin price volatility, rising network difficulty, energy costs, hardware obsolescence, regulatory changes, counterparty exposure to **Vancelian LTD** (the joint venture that operates the programme), and capital illiquidity through the engagement period (maturity 31 December 2028 for all new and migrated positions). A **partial or total loss of capital** is possible in adverse scenarios. For full details, always refer to the **CGUPM** (Terms & Conditions of the Mining Programme) and the official offer documentation.

## Details

### Understanding the nature of the product before reading the risks
Before going through each risk category, it helps to remember what Cloud Mining actually is: a **contract to purchase computing power** attached to an industrial mining operation. It is not a loan, not a savings deposit, and not a regulated investment product. What you buy is a share of the hashrate deployed by the programme, and what you receive is a share of the bitcoins that hashrate mines over the life of the contract. This nuance is what shapes every risk listed below — your returns move with the economics of mining, not with a fixed interest promise.

### The foundational risk — Bitcoin price
The single most important variable is the price of Bitcoin. Each day the machines mine a small fraction of a BTC, and your share of that production is converted and paid out in euros. When BTC rises, the euro value of the daily production rises with it; when BTC falls sharply, the euro value of your daily payouts falls mechanically — even if the hardware is performing perfectly and hashrate is stable. There is no smoothing mechanism, no floor, and no hedging layer between the BTC price and your yield.

### Network dynamics — difficulty, halving and energy
Three technical forces act on mining economics simultaneously, and they all converge on the same variable: the **hashprice** (the revenue generated per TH/s of computing power). First, **network difficulty** rises over time as more miners join the network, which mechanically reduces the share of blocks our hashrate captures. Second, the **Bitcoin halving** cuts block rewards in half roughly every four years, compressing the revenue side. Third, **energy costs** — even when operations are outsourced to sites running on hydro, gas recovery or load-balancing strategies — can drift upwards and squeeze the operator's margin. Together, these three forces mean the yield observed today is not a baseline for tomorrow.

### Counterparty risk — who stands behind the contract
This is the point most often misunderstood, so it deserves to be stated clearly. Your **contractual counterparty is Vancelian LTD**, a UAE-ADGM entity that operates the Cloud Mining programme as a joint venture between **Automata Group LTD (UK)** and **Hearst Solution FZCO**. Hearst is the technical operator on the ground, but it is Vancelian LTD that owes you the reimbursement of capital at the end of the contract. That ability to reimburse depends on the own funds and the financial capacity of Vancelian LTD, not on the goodwill of Hearst alone. See [[cloud-mining-who-reimburses-if-bankruptcy|Who reimburses in case of bankruptcy?]] for the detailed contractual framework under CGUPM article 4.3.

### Illiquidity — a deliberate structural choice
Cloud Mining is committed until contract maturity (31 December 2028 for all new and migrated positions; pre-migration contracts retain their original maturity, visible in your client account), with only limited early-exit windows. This is not an inconvenience — it is a deliberate design choice. Mining infrastructure only pays back its economics over a multi-year horizon, and stable hashrate is what allows the programme to negotiate favourable energy contracts and hardware deals. The trade-off for you as an investor is explicit: your capital is not available on demand during the commitment period. See [[cloud-mining-early-exit-and-transfers|Early exit and transfers]] for the exact window mechanics.

### Regulatory and jurisdictional exposure
The regulatory treatment of crypto-mining activity is still evolving across jurisdictions. Some countries have moved to restrict or ban the activity, others have tightened the framing of mining revenues, and there is a residual risk of international sanctions if infrastructure happens to be deployed in a sensitive zone. The programme's geographic diversification limits this exposure but does not neutralise it. Vancelian operates under MiCA in the EEA — see [[cloud-mining-mica-and-european-regulation|Cloud Mining and MiCA]] for the regulatory perimeter.

### Custody and technological backbone
Your crypto-assets are held in segregated wallets operated by Vancelian, with key security delivered through **Fireblocks MPC** technology. The robustness of fund security therefore depends on two layers: Vancelian's own custody operations, and the integrity of the underlying MPC infrastructure. Both layers are industry-standard, but neither is unconditional.

### What the programme does to mitigate — and what it cannot eliminate
Three operational mitigations sit in place: **geographic diversification** across Ethiopia, the United States and Kazakhstan to avoid single-site exposure; an **energy strategy** favouring low-cost and ideally renewable sources (hydro in Ethiopia and Norway, gas recovery, load balancing); and **technical operations** handled by Hearst Solution FZCO, which sources Bitmain hardware and runs 24/7 monitoring with rapid replacement of faulty machines. These mitigations materially reduce operational risk, but none of them addresses counterparty risk or market risk — those remain structural to the product.

### How to position Cloud Mining in a portfolio
Cloud Mining is a long-horizon, illiquid product exposed to BTC market dynamics. It is therefore best understood as an **allocation bucket** within a diversified wealth strategy — not as a short-term vehicle, not as a cash equivalent, and not as a regulated savings product. Past performance does not predict future results; advertised yields are always indicative. For the full contractual framework, refer to the **CGUPM** and the offer documentation available in the Vancelian app, or contact support@vancelian.com for questions specific to your situation.
