# Which is riskier — a Vault or an Exclusive Offer?

## Short answer

The risk profile is **structurally different** between the two products — there is no universal answer of "one is riskier than the other" because the answer depends on what you mean by risk.

A **Vault** spreads your capital across several underlyings (Cloud Mining, Solaria, EURC reserve). This diversification reduces your exposure to a single source of risk: if one underlying experiences a **counter-performance**, only part of your capital is exposed, and the allocation can be adapted to compensate. The trade-off is a more moderate indicative rate.

An **Exclusive Offer** concentrates your capital on a single project — execution, schedule, counterparty. Diversification does not cushion a counter-performance on this product; however, a margin of safety is built into each offer's Business Plan, designed to absorb measured variance. The trade-off is a more attractive indicative rate.

## Details

### How the risk is structured on a Vault

A Vault is a **diversified pocket** with allocations defined upfront at the moment of deposit. Diversification means that a counter-performance on one underlying does not translate into a proportional impact on your full capital — your exposure to any single source of risk is partial. In addition, Vancelian operates the allocation within the framework you accepted at subscription and **can adapt the allocation over time to compensate a counter-performance** on one underlying. The distributed rate is fixed, with small variations on the gross yield absorbed by Vancelian's intermediation margin — see [[vancelian-rate-smoothing-and-margin]].

For a full breakdown of vault risks by component, see [[are-there-any-risks-of-capital-loss]].

### How the risk is structured on an Exclusive Offer

An Exclusive Offer concentrates your participation on a single project. You are exposed to the specific risks of that project — for a real estate offer, this includes execution, sale or rental schedule, and the borrower SPV's ability to manage its treasury and meet its contractual commitments. Diversification does not soften a counter-performance.

What partially offsets this is the **margin of safety built into each offer's Business Plan**: each Exclusive Offer is structured with a contractual margin between the projected revenue and the rate distributed to clients. This margin is designed to absorb measured variance on the project execution. Beyond that margin, the residual risk is on the counterparty.

For Cloud Mining specifically, the structure is different (computing-power purchase contract, with the Vancelian LTD JV as counterparty) — see [[cloud-mining-risks-overview]]. For Dubai Villa Al Barari risks, see [[dubai-villa-risk-summary]].

### The structural difference

| | Vault | Exclusive Offer |
|---|---|---|
| **Capital exposure** | Spread across several underlyings | Concentrated on one project |
| **Counter-performance impact** | Partial; allocation can be adapted | Full on that offer; absorbed by BP margin up to its limit |
| **Counterparty** | Vancelian operates the allocation framework | Single named counterparty per offer |
| **Indicative rate** | Lower (5.1%–8.31% per Privilege Club tier) | Higher (e.g. 10.7%–11.5% Dubai Villa) — indicative |
| **Risk lever** | Diversification | BP margin + counterparty solidity |

The Vault enables **better management of a potential counter-performance** through diversification and allocation adaptability. The Exclusive Offer concentrates the participation on a single project — which is precisely why the indicative rate reflects this exposure.

### Residual risk applies to both

Neither product carries a formal capital guarantee. As with any financial product, **a residual risk is always present**. In the event of a counterparty default — whether a Vault underlying or an Exclusive Offer SPV — your capital is exposed. The mechanics, contractual commitments, and absolute cap of the residual risk are documented in the contractual documentation of each product (T&C, CGUPM for Cloud Mining, commercial brochure for each real estate offer).

Rates are indicative and may change between subscription windows — always verify the live rate in the Vancelian app.

## How to decide

Choosing between the two products is a **personal decision**, possibly informed by external personalised advice if you wish. The decision depends on:
- Your tolerance to capital concentration
- Your investment horizon
- Your appetite for the indicative rate

If you need to find the contractual documentation, the FAQ, or the T&C of any product, contact Vancelian support — they will help you locate the right resources. The complete information is available in the FAQ section and the T&C accessible directly in the Vancelian app.

## Caveats

- This page describes structural risk profiles, not a quantified risk hierarchy. Quantification depends on the specific offer, the market context, and the moment of subscription.
- Neither product offers a formal capital guarantee. Always read the contractual documentation before subscribing.
- Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future performance.
