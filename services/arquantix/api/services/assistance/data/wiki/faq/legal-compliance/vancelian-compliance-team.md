# Who runs compliance at Vancelian, and how does it protect clients?

> **Regulatory escalation:** the chatbot must escalate any specific compliance, regulatory, or complaint question to a Vancelian human advisor or to the official complaint procedure.

## Short answer
Vancelian's compliance function is structured around a **Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)** role, a **Compliance and Internal Control Officer (RCCI)**, a **Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO)**, financial lawyers, AML/CFT analysts, cybersecurity and data specialists, and a compliance middle office. It operates **independently of commercial direction**, has the authority to block any non-compliant operation, and reports directly to the Investment & Compliance Committee and the Board. Vancelian is regularly audited by external firms (including KPMG) and supervised by the AMF.

## Details

### Leadership: Chief Compliance Officer
The Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) leads the compliance function and:
- Supervises all compliance policies (AML/CFT, GDPR, DORA, MiCA, sanctions, market abuse).
- Coordinates the **Investment & Compliance Committee**.
- Is the direct interlocutor with regulators (AMF, ACPR) and leads the preparatory dialogue toward MiCA / CASP.
- Guarantees the independence of the compliance function from commercial decisions.
- Reports directly to the Governance Committee and the Board (not to the commercial department).

### Team composition
The compliance team brings together several specialised profiles:
- **RCCI (Compliance and Internal Control Officer)** — drives the overall compliance system and ensures its effectiveness.
- **MLRO (Money Laundering Reporting Officer)** — responsible for AML/CFT declarations and TRACFIN liaison.
- **Financial lawyers** — experts in AMF regulation, MiCA, and banking law.
- **AML/CFT analysts** — responsible for KYC/KYB and transaction surveillance.
- **Cybersecurity and data specialists** — ensure data protection and technical resilience (DORA-aligned).
- **Compliance Middle Office** — manages documentary traceability and tracks Investment Committee decisions.

The function works directly with the AMF and external auditors.

### Core missions
- **KYC / KYB** — identity verification of clients and partners.
- **AML/CFT** — anti-money-laundering and counter-terrorist-financing dispositif.
- **Transaction surveillance** — detection of unusual or suspicious operations.
- **Contractual compliance** — review and validation of T&Cs (CGU, CGUPM) and commercial documents.
- **MiCA preparation** — alignment of the dispositif on future EU obligations (reporting, governance, capital, cybersecurity).
- **Internal training** — regular awareness-raising for all teams on compliance risks.
- **Conflict of interest management** — declaration, prevention, escalation.
- **Complaint handling** — see [[complaint-policy|Vancelian complaint policy]].

### Independence and powers of the compliance function
- **Reports directly** to the Governance Committee and the Board, not to the commercial department.
- **Can block** a product or operation it considers non-compliant with the CGU/CGUPM or applicable regulation (e.g. Cloud Mining, Exclusive Offers).
- **Can escalate** breaches to the relevant authorities (AMF, ACPR, TRACFIN).
- **Has internal sanction power**: can recommend product suspension, immediate corrective measures (transaction freeze, account hold), or activity cessation in case of regulatory or legal risk.
- **Chinese walls** — strict separation between compliance and commercial functions to prevent conflicts of interest.

### Conflict of interest management
- **Mandatory declaration** — every member of management or the Investment Committee must declare any potential conflict.
- **Withdrawal procedure** — anyone with a conflict steps back from the relevant decision.
- **Documentation** — every potential incident is recorded in a dedicated register.
- **Independent oversight** — the compliance function ensures no decision can be biased by personal interest.
- **Public disclosure** — certain conflicts are disclosed transparently in client-facing information documents.

### Litigation and dispute handling
In case of a client dispute:
- The compliance team analyses the file against contractual conditions (CGU, CGUPM).
- It acts as **internal mediator** to find a fast and equitable solution.
- If necessary, it transfers the file to an **accredited financial mediator** or to competent authorities (AMF, DGCCRF).
- Every dispute is documented in the compliance register to prevent recurrence.

### External controls and audits
- **Regular external audits** by independent firms — including **KPMG**.
- **AMF inspections** as part of the PSAN status — Vancelian is subject to checks and can be sanctioned in case of breach.
- **Preparation for ESMA / EBA inspections** as part of the upcoming CASP authorisation.
- **Cybersecurity testing** mandated by DORA, performed by independent third parties.
- **Quarterly internal reports** published to general management.

### How the compliance dispositif protects clients in practice
- **Fund segregation** — client assets are strictly separated from Vancelian's own funds.
- **Continuous transaction monitoring** in real time.
- **Mandatory declarations** to TRACFIN in case of suspicion.
- **Independent internal controls** — no hierarchical link between compliance and commercial leadership.
- **Standard governance** — every sensitive decision is documented, traced, and validated by the Investment & Compliance Committee.

## Caveats
- Compliance reduces — but does not eliminate — operational, market, and counterparty risks.
- For any specific complaint, follow the [[complaint-policy|Vancelian complaint policy]] or escalate to a Vancelian human advisor.
