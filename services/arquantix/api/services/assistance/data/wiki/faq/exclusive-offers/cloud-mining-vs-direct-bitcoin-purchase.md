# Cloud Mining vs. buying Bitcoin directly: what's the difference?

## Short answer
Buying Bitcoin gives you **price exposure only** — your return depends entirely on how the BTC price moves. Cloud Mining gives you exposure to a **productive activity**: you earn daily rewards in EURC tied to mining output, but you also take on additional risks (network difficulty, energy costs, operator counterparty). The two are different profiles, with different horizons and different risks — they can be complementary in a portfolio, not direct substitutes.

## Details

### Direct Bitcoin purchase
- **Exposure**: BTC price only.
- **Returns**: capital appreciation if BTC rises (no yield in itself).
- **Liquidity**: full — sell any time on the Vancelian app.
- **Risk profile**: market volatility.

### Cloud Mining
- **Exposure**: BTC price **plus** network difficulty, energy costs, and operator efficiency.
- **Returns**: daily rewards in **EURC** (euro-pegged stablecoin), credited automatically.
- **Liquidity**: capital locked until contract maturity (**31 December 2028** for all new and migrated positions; pre-migration contracts retain their original maturity), with limited early-exit windows in May and November (matched-supply queue).
- **Risk profile**: market + operational + counterparty + illiquidity. See [[cloud-mining-risks-overview|Cloud Mining risks overview]].

### A useful analogy
Cloud Mining is like **owning a share of a productive factory** that mints Bitcoin. As long as the factory is profitable, it generates a margin (the rewards). It is **not** a financial product or a speculative bet on price — it is exposure to the economics of running mining hardware. Buying BTC directly is closer to owning the commodity itself.

### Why an investor might choose one over the other (or both)
- **Choose direct BTC** if: you want maximum liquidity, simple price exposure, and a short or flexible horizon.
- **Choose Cloud Mining** if: you want a daily yield stream tied to a productive activity, you're comfortable with a 4-year horizon, and you accept variable returns.
- **Combine both** if: you want diversification within your Bitcoin exposure — direct BTC for liquidity, Cloud Mining for productive yield.

## Caveats
- Cloud Mining is **not** a fixed-yield product. Rewards vary with the four drivers covered in [[cloud-mining-yield-factors|yield factors]].
- Don't compare Cloud Mining APR to a savings account — the risk profile is fundamentally different.
