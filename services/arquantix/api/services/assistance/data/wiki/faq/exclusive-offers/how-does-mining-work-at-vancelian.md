# How does mining work at Vancelian?

## Short answer
Bitcoin mining validates transactions, creates new bitcoins, and secures the network through proof of work. Vancelian's partner Hearst operates physical mining infrastructure (next-generation Bitmain machines in international data centres), and you purchase a share of that computing power. You receive a daily reward in **EURC** (a euro stablecoin) credited to your "Available" wallet. The reward is **variable** — never guaranteed — because it depends on Bitcoin price, network difficulty, energy costs, and machine availability.

## Details

### Why mining is essential to the Bitcoin network
Mining performs three core functions: it **validates transactions**, **creates new bitcoins**, and **secures the network** via proof of work. Each miner contributes computing power (hashrate); the more hashrate, the harder it becomes for an attacker to compromise the chain.

### How a Cloud Mining contract translates into rewards
1. You subscribe to the offer. Your funds are routed to your **Power wallet** — the wallet dedicated to the computing power allocated to your contract by **Vancelian LTD (UAE-ADGM)**, which operates the programme as counterparty. **Hearst Solution FZCO** runs the physical infrastructure.
2. That hashrate participates in the Bitcoin network alongside Hearst's full fleet.
3. The fleet generates BTC rewards (block subsidy + transaction fees).
4. BTC rewards are **automatically converted to EURC** and credited **every day** to your "**Available**" wallet — withdrawable at any time.

### What drives the reward (and why it varies)
The daily reward depends on:
- **Bitcoin price** — higher BTC mechanically increases the EUR value of mined rewards.
- **Network difficulty** — adjusts roughly every 2 weeks (every 2,016 blocks) to keep block time near 10 minutes. As more efficient machines join the network, difficulty rises and the share of reward per unit of hashrate falls.
- **Energy cost** — the dominant operating cost. Sites are chosen to access cheap, stable, ideally low-carbon power.
- **Machine efficiency** — newer ASICs (e.g. Bitmain Antminer S21 Pro+) deliver more TH/s per watt.
- **Bitcoin halving** — every ~4 years the block subsidy is cut in half, mechanically halving the BTC reward per unit of hashrate. See [[cloud-mining-bitcoin-halving-impact|Bitcoin halving impact]].

### What Vancelian does (and doesn't) do
**Vancelian LTD (UAE-ADGM)** acts as the **contractual counterparty** of the Cloud Mining programme. **Automata France SAS** operates as the **PSAN-regulated intermediary** for European clients (subscription flow, EURC custody, app display). Their joint responsibility covers proper contract management and transparency of information. They **cannot guarantee a target rate** and are not liable for performance fluctuations tied to the mining activity itself. Hearst Solution FZCO handles infrastructure: sourcing, deployment, monitoring 24/7, and maintenance.

## Caveats
- The reward is **variable and not guaranteed**. Capital is **locked until contract maturity (31 December 2028 for all new and migrated positions)**, with limited early-exit windows — see [[cloud-mining-early-exit-and-transfers|Early exit and transfers]]. Pre-migration contracts retain their original maturity, visible in your client account.
- Cloud Mining is a **long-horizon, illiquid product**. Don't invest funds you may need short-term.
- See [[cloud-mining-risks-overview|Cloud Mining risks overview]] for the full risk picture.
