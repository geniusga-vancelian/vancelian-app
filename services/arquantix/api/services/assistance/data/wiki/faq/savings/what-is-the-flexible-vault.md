# What is the Flexible Vault and how does it work?

## Short answer
The Flexible Vault is Vancelian's open-ended savings product: a **diversified pocket** in which your funds are allocated across underlyings **defined upfront at the moment of deposit**. Daily returns are automatically reinvested into your deposit (compound interest), and you can withdraw your funds at any time. The pocket includes mining (Cloud Mining), the Solaria exclusive offer, and a EURC stablecoin reserve.

## Details
Our Flexible Vault offers a more innovative and diversified approach, with the potential for higher returns by leveraging the opportunities offered by digital assets and our mining program.

When you place funds in this solution, **the daily returns** you receive are automatically added to your initial deposit, allowing you to benefit from **attractive compound returns** over the long term.

Compound interest refers to returns calculated both on the initial capital and on the returns already accumulated. In other words, your interest isn't just earned on your original deposit but also on the interest that has already been credited. With Vancelian, interest is calculated daily, helping you **maximise your gains.**

You're free to **withdraw your funds at any time.**

### Allocation
Your savings are allocated within the Flexible Vault according to a **pre-defined allocation set at the moment of deposit**:

- Mining (or [Cloud Mining](https://vancelian.com/en/exclusives-offers))
- Our [Exclusive Offer](https://vancelian.com/en/exclusives-offers) Solaria
- A reserve held in EURC (a stablecoin tied to the euro)

*The EURC reserve is **non-remunerated** — as a euro stablecoin under **MiCA**, EURC cannot be deployed in yield-bearing activities without explicit regulatory approval. The reserve ensures liquidity for deposits and withdrawals. See [[how-vault-liquidity-and-returns-work]] for the full vault mechanic.*

The allocation may evolve over time as Vancelian rebalances between the EURC pocket and the yield-generating underlyings. The **rate distributed to clients is fixed**, and small variations on the gross yield generated by the allocation are absorbed by Vancelian's intermediation margin — see [[vancelian-rate-smoothing-and-margin]].

You can view your Vault's allocation at any time by going to your Flexible Vault, tapping the three dots in the top right corner, then selecting **More details about the offer.** The allocation history is also available in this section.

## How it works — EURC in and out
Your deposits and withdrawals are in **EURC** (a stablecoin pegged to the euro). EURC is the entry and exit point. Behind the scenes, your EURC is allocated across several yield-generating underlyings (Cloud Mining, Exclusive Offers, EURC reserve) according to the pre-defined allocation, and you are **never directly exposed to cryptocurrency price volatility**. You deposit in EURC and you withdraw in EURC. Vancelian operates the allocation and the rebalancing within the framework you accepted at subscription.

## Risks
Like any product that delivers a return, the Flexible Vault carries risk. The risks are identified and structurally limited: they relate primarily to the performance of the underlying allocations (Cloud Mining, Exclusive Offers) and, in an extreme theoretical case, to the stability of the EURC stablecoin. The vault is not allocated at any point to volatile crypto-assets such as BTC or ETH. Vancelian can adjust the allocation over time to maintain control over the product's trajectory. For a full breakdown by component, see [[are-there-any-risks-of-capital-loss]]. For vault mechanics and why the Flexible Vault typically yields less than the Future Vault, see [[how-vault-liquidity-and-returns-work]].

To create a Flexible Vault, go to **Home > Savings > Flexible Vault > Open**. Minimum deposit: **1 EUR** or approximately **10 USDC**. For step-by-step instructions, see [[how-do-i-create-a-flexible-vault]].
